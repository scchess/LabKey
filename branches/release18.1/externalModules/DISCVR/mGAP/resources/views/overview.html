Rhesus macaques are a powerful, yet often underserved genetic model. mGAP seeks to fill several major voids to promote the study of human genetic disease in this key pre-clinical model. The database will provide open access to genomic and phenotypic data from the large, pedigreed colony of Indian-origin rhesus macaques housed at the Oregon National Primate Research Center (ONPRC), ultimately including data from thousands of animals. These animals are genetically representative of major captive research populations.
Our goal is to provide a high-quality and useful dataset. Sequence data are analyzed using a vetted pipeline designed to produce high-confidence genotype calls. The resulting variants are annotated using a wide range of data sources, including predicted function, overlap with regulatory elements and association with phenotypes and diseases.

Explore Macaque Genetic Variation:

  • Our variant catalog provides a survey of genetic variants identified in macaques. These are heavily annotated, viewable through our genome browser, and can be downloaded.
  • Researchers can quickly view known variants within gene(s) of interest using the 'Gene Search' box or through our genome browser. Search capabilities will be expanded throughout this project.
  • As the project evolves, we will ultimately provide tools for researchers to identify specific animals with variants of interest.

Resources provided by mGAP:

  • Rhesus Macaque Variant Catalog: mGAP provides a curated and annotated list of macaque SNVs, generated from our cohort
  • Raw Sequence Data: whole genome and genotyping-by-sequencing (GBS) data are available for all animals
  • Genotype Data: genotypes will be available for all animals in Q4 2017
  • Phenotypic and Clinical Data: will be available in 2018
